# UNIT 10: Shopping & Quantities - Complete Vocab Cards
**Function:** Shopping transactions, quantifiers for countable/uncountable nouns, FOR phrases  
**Main Grammar:** a few / a bit of / a lot of, FOR phrases (price, purpose, deadline, fame, definition)  
**Uzbek:** Xarid va miqdorlar, FOR bilan iboralar

---

## LESSON 1: Shopping Preferences & FOR Phrases (Purpose)

### Dialogue 1: Nilufar & Bekzod - At the Grocery Shop
**Context:** Two friends shopping for groceries  
**Location:** Grocery shop  
**Time:** Saturday morning

**Nilufar:** Salom Bekzod! Sen nima sotib olayapsan?  
Hi Bekzod! What are you buying?

**Bekzod:** Men pechenye sotib olayapman. Nonushta uchun.  
I'm buying biscuits. For breakfast.

**Nilufar:** Yaxshi! Men pishloq sotib olayapman. Tushlik uchun.  
Good! I'm buying cheese. For lunch.

**Bekzod:** Zo'r! Bu sirg'alar-chi? Kimga?  
Great! What about these earrings? For whom?

**Nilufar:** Bu sirg'alar onam uchun. Uning tug'ilgan kuni uchun.  
These earrings are for my mother. For her birthday.

**Bekzod:** Chiroyli! Bu hamyon seniki-mi?  
Beautiful! Is this wallet yours?

**Nilufar:** Ha, bu hamyon meniki. Men uni kecha sotib oldim.  
Yes, this wallet is mine. I bought it yesterday.

**Bekzod:** Yaxshi tanlov! Endi to'lash vaqti!  
Good choice! Now it's time to pay!

### VOCAB CARD 1: biscuit(s)

**Uzbek Context Question:**  
Sen nonushtaga hech narsa yemaysanmi? Faqat choy ichasanmi?  
You don't eat anything for breakfast? You only drink tea?

**Uzbek Mirror Answer:**  
Yo'q, hech narsa emas. Men pechenye yeyapman.  
No, not nothing. I'm eating biscuits.

**Hybrid Answer (Uzbek + English):**  
Yo'q, men **biscuits** yeyapman. Pechenyelar.  
No, I'm eating **biscuits**. Biscuits.

**ANCHOR SENTENCE (from dialogue):**  
Men pechenye sotib olayapman. ‚Üí I'm buying **biscuits**.

**Example 2 (P2_UNIT - Quantifier):**  
I have a few **biscuits**.  
Menda biroz pechenyelar bor.

**Example 3 (P3_RECYCLE - With U01-U09 Vocab):**  
I bought a few **biscuits** for breakfast yesterday because I love them.  
Men kecha nonushta uchun biroz pechenye sotib oldim chunki men ularni yaxshi ko'raman.

## LESSON 2: Past Shopping Transactions & Quantifiers

### Dialogue 2: Madina & Otabek - After Shopping
**Context:** Two friends meeting after shopping  
**Location:** Coffee shop  
**Time:** Saturday afternoon

**Madina:** Salom Otabek! Sen nima sotib oldingiz?  
Hi Otabek! What did you buy?

**Otabek:** Men biroz pechenye sotib oldim. Bir nechta.  
I bought some biscuits. A few.

**Madina:** Yaxshi! Men biroz pishloq sotib oldim. Ozgina.  
Good! I bought some cheese. A bit.

**Otabek:** Sen biroz pul sarfladingizmi? Ko'pmi?  
Did you spend some money? A lot?

**Madina:** Yo'q, ko'p emas. Men ozgina pul sarfladim.  
No, not a lot. I spent a bit of money.

**Otabek:** Men ham. Men bir nechta tanga oldim.  
Me too. I received a few coins.

**Madina:** Chek oldingizmi?  
Did you get the receipt?

**Otabek:** Ha, men chekni oldim. U meniki.  
Yes, I got the receipt. It's mine.

### VOCAB CARD 16: Countable vs Uncountable

**Uzbek Context Question:**  
Men "a few money" deya olamanmi? Nima uchun yo'q?  
Can I say "a few money"? Why not?

**Uzbek Mirror Answer:**  
Yo'q, "money" sanalmaydigan. "A bit of money" deyiladi.  
No, "money" is uncountable. You say "a bit of money".

**Hybrid Answer (Uzbek + English):**  
"**Countable**" ‚Üí a few coins. "**Uncountable**" ‚Üí a bit of money.  
"Sanaladi" ‚Üí a few tangalar. "Sanalmaydigan" ‚Üí a bit of pul.

**ANCHOR SENTENCE (from dialogue):**  
(Extended example)  
A few biscuits (countable) vs A bit of cheese (uncountable)

**Example 2 (P2_UNIT - More Examples):**  
**Countable:** a few earrings / **Uncountable:** a bit of jewelry.  
Sanaladi: biroz sirg'alar / Sanalmaydigan: ozgina taqinchoqlar.

**Example 3 (P3_RECYCLE - With U01-U09 Vocab):**  
I bought a few **countable** items (biscuits, coins) and a bit of **uncountable** items (cheese, money) yesterday.  
Men kecha biroz sanaladigan narsalar (pechenyelar, tangalar) va ozgina sanalmaydigan narsalar (pishloq, pul) sotib oldim.

## LESSON 3: Prices & FOR Phrases (Amount/Price/Fame/Definition)

### Dialogue 3: Gulnora & Sardor - Shopping Mall
**Context:** Two friends shopping and comparing prices  
**Location:** Shopping mall  
**Time:** Sunday afternoon

**Gulnora:** Salom Sardor! Bu sumka qancha?  
Hi Sardor! How much is this bag?

**Sardor:** Bu sumka 50 dollarga. Juda qimmat!  
This bag is for 50 dollars. Very expensive!

**Gulnora:** Juda qimmat! Men eski sumkamni 20 dollarga sotdim.  
Very expensive! I sold my old bag for 20 dollars.

**Sardor:** Yaxshi! Bu ish dushanba kuni uchun tugashi kerak.  
Good! This work must be finished for Monday.

**Gulnora:** Aytgancha, Ispaniya ovqati bilan mashhur.  
By the way, Spain is famous for food.

**Sardor:** Ha! Va "Sneakers" - bu "trainers" uchun so'z.  
Yes! And "Sneakers" - this is the word for "trainers".

**Gulnora:** To'g'ri! Men chegirma uchun so'rayman.  
Right! I'll ask for a discount.

**Sardor:** Yaxshi fikr! Chekni oling!  
Good idea! Get the receipt!
