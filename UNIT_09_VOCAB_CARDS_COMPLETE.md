# UNIT 09: Clothes & Ownership - Complete Vocab Cards
**Function:** Identifying clothing items and materials, expressing ownership with possessive pronouns  
**Main Grammar:** Possessive Pronouns (mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs), be + Material  
**Uzbek:** Kiyimlar va materiallar, egalik olmoshlari

---

## LESSON 1: Basic Clothing Items

### Dialogue 1: Malika & Javohir - Getting Ready for School
**Context:** Two siblings getting ready for school in the morning  
**Location:** At home, in the bedroom  
**Time:** Monday morning

**Malika:** Salom Javohir! Mening jemperim qayerda?  
Hi Javohir! Where is my jumper?

**Javohir:** Bilmayman. Bu seniki emasmikan?  
I don't know. Isn't this yours?

**Malika:** Yo'q, bu meniki emas. Bu uniki. Opamniki.  
No, this isn't mine. It's hers. Our sister's.

**Javohir:** Tushundim. Mening krossovkalarim qayerda?  
I understand. Where are my trainers?

**Malika:** Eshik oldida. Bu krossovkalar seniki.  
By the door. These trainers are yours.

**Javohir:** Rahmat! Sen sharf kiysanmi?  
Thanks! Are you wearing a scarf?

**Malika:** Ha, bu sharf meniki. Men uni yaxshi ko'raman.  
Yes, this scarf is mine. I love it.

**Javohir:** Chiroyli! Endi maktabga boramiz!  
Beautiful! Now let's go to school!

## LESSON 2: Materials & Possessive Pronouns

### Dialogue 2: Dilorom & Aziz - Shopping for Clothes
**Context:** Two friends shopping for clothes at a store  
**Location:** Clothing shop  
**Time:** Saturday afternoon

**Dilorom:** Salom Aziz! Bu etiklar chiroyli! Nechadan?

**Aziz:** Bilmayman. Bu etiklar qanday materialdan?  
I don't know. What material are these boots?

**Dilorom:** Bu etiklar charmdan. Juda sifatli!

**Aziz:** Ajoyib! Bu jemper-chi? Nechadan?  
Wonderful! What about this jumper? How much is it?

**Dilorom:** Bu jemper jundan. U issiq.  
This jumper is wool. It's warm.

**Aziz:** Yaxshi! Men sotib olaman. Bu meniki bo'ladi!  
Good! I'll buy it. This will be mine!

**Dilorom:** Men ham etik sotib olaman. Ular meniki bo'ladi!  
I'll also buy the boots. They'll be mine!

**Aziz:** Zo'r! Endi to'lash vaqti!  
Great! Now it's time to pay!

## LESSON 3: Ownership Questions with Whose

### Dialogue 3: Feruza & Shohruh - Lost and Found
**Context:** Two classmates at the school lost and found office  
**Location:** School office  
**Time:** After classes

**Feruza:** Salom Shohruh! Bu sharf kimniki?  
Hi Shohruh! Whose scarf is this?

**Shohruh:** Bilmayman. Bu meniki emas. Balki uniki?  
I don't know. It's not mine. Maybe it's hers?

**Feruza:** Yo'q, bu uniki emas. U sharf kiymaydi.  
No, it's not hers. She doesn't wear scarves.

**Shohruh:** Bu qo'lqoplar-chi? Kimniki?  
What about these gloves? Whose are they?

**Feruza:** Menimcha bu qo'lqoplar bizniki. Bizning sinfdaniki.  
I think these gloves are ours. From our class.

**Shohruh:** Ajoyib! Va bu kepka kimniki?  
Wonderful! And whose cap is this?

**Feruza:** Bu kepka ularniki. Men ko'rdim, ular bugun kiyganlar.  
This cap is theirs. I saw them wearing it today.

**Shohruh:** Yaxshi! Endi hamma narsani egalariga qaytaramiz!  
Good! Now we'll return everything to their owners!

### VOCAB CARD 23: Possessive Determiner → Possessive Pronoun

**Uzbek Context Question:**  
Men "mening kitobim" deb aytmoqchiman. Qanday qilib qisqartira olaman?  
I want to say "my book". How can I shorten it?

**Uzbek Mirror Answer:**  
Agar kitobni aytish kerak bo'lmasa, "meniki" deb aytasiz.  
If you don't need to say the book, you say "mine".

**Hybrid Answer (Uzbek + English):**  
"My book" → "**mine**". Mening kitobim → meniki.  
"My book" → "**mine**". My book → mine.

**ANCHOR SENTENCE (from dialogue):**  
Mening jemperim → Bu meniki. → My jumper → This is **mine**.

**Example 2 (P2_UNIT - Different Form):**

### VOCAB CARD 24: Material as Predicate Adjective

**Uzbek Context Question:**
